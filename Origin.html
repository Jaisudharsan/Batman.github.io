Batman's Origin

I was born into the luxury and privilege of Gotham City's elite, enjoying a carefree childhood with my parents, Thomas and Martha Wayne. Our lives seemed set on a path of ease and security, shielded by our family’s wealth and status. But everything changed one fateful night. As we left a movie theater in one of Gotham’s rougher neighborhoods, our lives were violently interrupted. I watched helplessly as my parents were murdered in a brutal mugging, leaving me orphaned and thrust into the care of our loyal butler, Alfred Pennyworth.

The loss of my parents was the catalyst that transformed my grief into an unyielding resolve. Instead of succumbing to despair, I vowed to dedicate my life to fighting the crime that had stolen my family from me. My mission became clear: I would protect Gotham City from the same darkness that had claimed my parents. My fear of bats, which had haunted me as a child, became the inspiration for my new identity. I chose to embody the bat—a symbol of both fear and hope—to wage a relentless war against the criminals who plague my city.

As Batman, I am summoned by the Bat-Signal, a beacon used by my ally, Commissioner Jim Gordon, to call upon me in times of crisis. From the shadows, I watch over Gotham, a vigilant guardian and a symbol of justice. My presence is both a source of hope for the innocent and a warning to those who seek to disrupt the peace. Through my efforts, I strive to ensure that no one else will have to experience the same pain and loss that I endured.
